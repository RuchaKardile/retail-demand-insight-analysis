# Retail Demand & Merchandising Insight Analysis

## Overview
This project is a field-based retail analysis conducted across Zara, H&M, and Westside stores in Pune, India, during the December festive–winter period.

The objective was to understand how merchandising, pricing, and display strategies translate into demand signals — and how a Business Analyst would convert these observations into data questions and system-level insights.

## Scope
- Brands: Zara, H&M, Westside
- Category: Women’s wear
- Location: Pune, Maharashtra, India
- Method: Store walkthrough observation (no internal sales data)

## Key Questions Explored
- How can store-level signals help anticipate demand patterns?
- What merchandising decisions influence sell-through and conversion?
- What analytics and system data would support better retail decisions?

## Approach
- Observed customer behavior, stock availability, and display placement
- Compared pricing and product mix across brands
- Identified demand patterns and product gaps
- Translated observations into business hypotheses and data requirements

## BA / Analytics Lens
This project focuses on:
- Problem framing
- Hypothesis-driven thinking
- Retail domain understanding
- Translating business observations into analytics questions

## Deliverables
- Store walkthrough analysis (PDF)
- Comparative insights across three retail brands
- Business and data questions for retail analytics teams

## Disclaimer
This project is for educational and portfolio purposes only. All observations are based on publicly visible in-store information. No confidential or proprietary data was used.
